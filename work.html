How Plagiarism Checker works?

Our Plagiarism Checker utilizes TF-IDF vectorization combined with cosine similarity to analyze text similarity effectively. Here's a brief overview of how it works:

1. TF-IDF Vectorization

TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) is a statistical measure used to evaluate the importance of a word in a document relative to a collection of documents (corpus). The process involves:

  • Term Frequency (TF): This measures how frequently a term appears in a document. The more times a word appears, the higher its TF value.
  • Inverse Document Frequency (IDF): This measures how important a term is across all documents in the corpus. Rare terms across documents get higher IDF values.
  • Vectorization: Each document is transformed into a vector in a multi-dimensional space, where each dimension corresponds to a unique term from the corpus, weighted by its TF-IDF score.

2. Cosine Similarity

Cosine similarity is a metric used to measure how similar two vectors are, regardless of their magnitude. It calculates the cosine of the angle between two vectors in a multi-dimensional space. The steps include:

  • Vector Comparison: Once the documents are vectorized using TF-IDF, cosine similarity compares these vectors.
  • Similarity Score: The cosine similarity score ranges from -1 to 1, where 1 indicates identical documents, 0 indicates no similarity, and -1 indicates completely opposite documents.

3. Plagiarism Detection

By combining TF-IDF vectorization and cosine similarity, our Plagiarism Checker can effectively identify similarities between the user's text and reference documents. A high cosine similarity score suggests potential plagiarism, allowing users to take appropriate actions.

This method provides a robust and efficient way to detect text similarity, making it a valuable tool for educators, writers, and researchers.
